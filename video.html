Video Compression

Video is essentially a sequence of images (frames) played very fast. Video compression relies heavily on "Temporal Redundancy".

Instead of saving 60 full images for every second of video, formats like MP4/H.264 save one full "Keyframe" (I-frame) and then only record the differences or movements in the subsequent frames (P-frames). If a news anchor is talking, only their lips and face movements are saved; the background desk and wall are reused from the first frame.

Videos are just lots of pictures moving really fast! 🎬

If we want to film a news reporter on TV, the background wall doesn't move. So, the computer takes ONE picture of the wall, and then only draws the reporter's mouth moving! It doesn't waste time drawing the wall over and over again. This is called saving only the changes!
