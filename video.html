Video encoding (like H.264 or HEVC) is arguably the most complex form of compression. Because video is just a rapid sequence of images, it contains massive Temporal Redundancy (data that doesn't change between frames). Encoders use Motion Estimation algorithms to track moving blocks of pixels rather than redrawing them.

🔬 Deep Dive: I, P, and B Frames (GOP)

Video streams are organized into a Group of Pictures (GOP). I-frames (Intra) are full images (like a JPEG). P-frames (Predicted) only store changes from the previous frame. B-frames (Bi-directional) are the most efficient: they calculate pixel movements by looking at both past AND future frames to predict the current frame's content.
