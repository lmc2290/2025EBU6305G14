Audio Compression

Audio compression, particularly formats like MP3, utilizes a biological phenomenon called "Psychoacoustics".

The human ear cannot hear certain extremely high or low frequencies. Additionally, if a loud sound (like a drum beat) plays at the exact same time as a quiet sound (like a whisper), our ears only perceive the drum. The compression algorithm simply deletes the whisper and the inaudible frequencies, reducing file size dramatically without the listener noticing.

Computers trick our ears to save space! 🎧

Imagine a giant drum banging right next to someone whispering. You wouldn't hear the whisper at all! The computer knows this, so it literally deletes the whisper from the song file. Because you couldn't hear it anyway, the song sounds exactly the same to you, but the file is much smaller!
