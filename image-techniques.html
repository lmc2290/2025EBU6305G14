Image Compression Techniques

Images contain thousands or millions of pixels. Storing every single one is inefficient. Algorithms like JPEG and PNG use "Spatial Redundancy" to compress images.

For example, if an image has a large blue sky, instead of saving "blue, blue, blue..." a thousand times, the computer saves the instruction: "1,000 pixels of this exact shade of blue". Furthermore, lossy formats like JPEG discard high-frequency color variations that the human eye is biologically poor at detecting.

How does a computer shrink a big photo? By grouping things! 🖼️

If you take a picture of a clear blue sky, the computer doesn't color every single tiny dot one by one. Instead, it acts like a smart painter and says: "Just paint this whole top half blue!" 🖍️ It also throws away tiny color differences that our eyes can't even notice anyway.
